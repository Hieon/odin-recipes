Grill Caesar Salad

This recipe, from Alan Ashkinaze of the now-closed Millesime in Manhattan, came to The Times in 2012. In his version of the classic Caesar salad, a light grilling enhances the flavor of the romaine lettuce, which is then brushed with a dressing brightened by lime juice. The whole thing is finished with Parmesan, toasted on the grill. It comes together quickly, and it’s a perfect pairing for a rib-eye, served along with a deep red. —Sam Sifton

Ingredients

  • 2 Heads of Romaine Lettuce
  • Salt
  • Pepper
  • 1 Tbsp of Grapeseed Oil

Caesar Dressing

  • 1/2 cup Raw Cashews
  • 1/2 cup Raw Sunflower Seeds
  • 1/3 cup Lemon Juice
  • 1/3 cup Nutrtional Yeast
  • 1/3 cup Parmesan shreds
  • 2 cloves of Garlic
  • 1 Tbsp of Dijon Mustard
  • 2 tsp of Vegan Worcestshire Sauce
  • Peppr to taste
  • 1/2 cup Water

Toppings

  • Croutons
  • Grated Parmesan

Steps

  1. Make the Caesar dressing by soaking the Cashews and Sunflower Seeds in hot water for at least 30 minutes.
  2. Blend the rinsed Cashews and Seeds with the rest of the dressing ingredients until sooth and creamy. Refrigerate until needed.
  3. Slice the Romaine Hearts in half lengthwise and brush the cut side with a bit of Oil then add Salt & Pepper.
  4. Place the Romaine Hearts cut side down onto a hot grill or grill pan for 3-4 minutes. You want the beautiful charred grill marks while keeping the rest of the lettuce crisp.
  5. Serve 2 halves on each plate with a generous drizzle of Caesar Dressing and top with Croutons and grated Parmesan.
